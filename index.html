Tutorial

Curious About How To Meditate?

Do Not Disturb

Absolute silence isn't necessary to meditate, but do your best to find a space where you won't be interrupted.

Sit Comfortably

Grab a comfy chair, sit up straight, and most of all, sit in a way that makes it easy to relax.

Clear Your Mind

As you meditate, thoughts will naturally pop up in your mind. That's fine - when a thought comes, gently let it go and return to silence.

Meditation Timer

How To Use Zen Timer

If you've heard that meditating is sitting in silence, then you're on the right track. But it's more than simply sitting quietly - it's about quieting your mind as well. In other words, it's not going to do you much good to sit quietly while your mind is still running at full speed.

When you begin your meditation, take a few deep breaths and then gently let go of any thoughts, feelings, worries, or emotions that are running through your head. If the thoughts or feelings come back, that's fine. Simply let go of them again.

If you've never meditated before and would like to begin, we recommend spending 5 minutes in silence every day. A short, daily habit of meditation will produce far more benefits than a long session done on occasion.

Zen Timer

Zen Timer uses soft chimes, placed at intervals during your meditation, to remind you to let go of any thoughts you may have, and quietly return to the center of your mind.

With practice, you'll be able to sit quietly, watching your thoughts come and go, without feeling any need to interact with them.
